Wild Jobi

About this work

Through a hybrid photographic workflow, Wild Jobi investigates the psychological landscapes of the "in-between." His work utilizes both traditional analog formats and modern digital tools to capture the atmospheric qualities of transition. This interplay of mediums reflects a deep interest in the concepts of object relations and psychological boundaries. Each image serves as a study of how environments function as "holding worlds," inviting the viewer to engage in the delicate play between subjective imagination and objective observation.

In-Between Worlds:
A Study of Potential Space.

This series explores the concept of "Transitional Spaces," drawing inspiration from the psychoanalytic theories of D.W. Winnicott. Through the lens, I seek to capture the "potential space"—that third area of human experience which lies between the inner psychic reality and the external, shared world. These photographs inhabit the thresholds: the quiet corners where imagination meets reality, and where the self begins to negotiate its place within the environment. By documenting these intermediate states, the project invites the viewer to linger in the ambiguity of the "not-me," reflecting on the delicate balance between holding on and letting go.
